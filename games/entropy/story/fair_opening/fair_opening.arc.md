# Arc — Opening Day (`fair_opening`)

Owned by the **Story Director**; beats machine-checked by the canon
gate (`scripts/14_story_director.py check fair_opening`). Scene:
`vaporis_fair`. Canon consulted: bible R-001…R-004.

## Premise

It is the first morning of La Foire Vaporum, and the great wheel — the
thing every visitor comes to see — will not turn. A pressure valve
seized overnight, and Magister Brontes is too proud to shout about it.
The player walks in with the morning crowd, hears the barker promise a
first turn at dusk that the wheel cannot deliver, and ends up being the
one who quietly threads the fair — machine show to midway to terrace —
to get it moving before the Rector's opening word.

## Stakes

Small and warm, deliberately: no villain, no doom. The fair's honor,
one apprentice's grade, and whether dusk arrives with a turning wheel
or an excruciating speech. This is the arc that teaches the player the
fair's geography — every beat pulls them through a different zone.

## Shape (five beats)

1. **Through the Porta** — admission at the gate; Prefect Cassia sets
   the tone of the Order's rules.
2. **The Barker's Promise** — the grand court at full noise; the
   promise of the first turn at dusk, and the first hint it's in doubt.
3. **The Seized Valve** — the machine show; Brontes needs a bronze
   valve, won't leave his boiler, and his apprentice is too junior to
   haggle.
4. **The Vendor's Price** — the midway; vendor games, a small trade,
   the valve changes hands.
5. **The First Turn** — the wheel terrace at dusk; the valve seats, the
   wheel turns, the crowd gathers below.
