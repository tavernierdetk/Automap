# The lore bible — game `entropy`

Owned by the **Lore Keeper** (docs/studio-org.md). This file is the
canon for humans; `canon.json` beside it is the registry the canon gate
reads. The two never disagree: every named entity here has a row there.
Entities enter as `proposed` and are promoted to `canon` by the keeper;
**rulings are append-only** — a ruling is amended by a later ruling,
never edited away.

The game currently holds two regions with two fictions:

---

## Region: `vaporis` — ours

**The world.** Roman steampunk. Pale marble and terracotta, bronze
steam pipes and verdigris, lawns quartered by pavement, statuary of
philosopher-engineers, steam over everything. Sunlit but hazy.

**The Order.** The faction that runs the Lyceum Vaporum — scholars of
the arcane-mechanical arts who have never known the difference between
a carnival and an engineering exhibition. Apprentices rivet the fair's
rides together as coursework.

### Places

- **Lyceum Vaporum** — the school campus (scene to come; brief drafted
  in docs/explorations/vaporis-lyceum.md). Its own geography is canon:
  Porta Fumaria (the gate), Via Triumphalis (the avenue), the
  Peristylium with the **Fountain of Minerva**, the Aqueduct of the
  Alchemists, the Fabrica (workshop), Hortus Arcanus (botanical court),
  Turris Astrorum (observatory), Atrium Magnum (the great hall).
- **La Foire Vaporum** — the annual fair on the great lawn west of the
  campus (`vaporis_fair`, live). Once a year, one week; half carnival,
  half exhibition. The **great wheel** turns over everything from its
  raised terrace and is the landmark navigated by from every corner.

### People

- **Rector Aurelia Fumans** — head of the Order. Seen at ceremonies;
  opens the fair each year.
- **Prefect Cassia** — a senior student with gate duty at the fair;
  earnest, rulebook-first.
- **Magister Brontes** — the mechanicus who runs the machine show;
  gruff, oil-stained, proud of his apprentices.
- **Minerva Machinatrix** — the Founder. Long dead; present as statues
  (hedge garden at the fair, founder statue at the Turris) and as the
  fountain's namesake. Retired from casting — she appears in stone and
  story only.

**The fair's people** (admitted 2026-07-18 with the first casting
sheet): Porter Felix (tickets, counts everything twice), Vox the
Barker (promises first, checks later), Prisca (sweets) and Naso
(salvage — parts from last year's coursework) on the midway, Druso the
wheel operator, Mirella the carousel keeper, Pippa (a child, always
one token short), Bassa the cook (feeds apprentices on credit),
Professor Gaius (off duty, grading nothing today), Tullo (Brontes'
apprentice — the boiler is his graded work), Naia (watches the pond
instead of the fair), Silvo (keeper of the hedge garden). Background
students Aulus, Livia, Metto, Pella, Varro and Flora walk the grounds
uncanonized — names on creature documents only.

### Rulings (append-only)

- **R-001 (2026-07-18).** Vaporis technology is steam and clockwork
  only — no gunpowder, no electricity. Light is flame; power is
  pressure.
- **R-002 (2026-07-18).** Proper nouns in vaporis take Latin-flavored
  forms (Lyceum Vaporum, Porta Fumaria). Everyday speech is plain.
- **R-003 (2026-07-18).** The fair runs one week a year; its rides are
  apprentice coursework. The machines on display are graded work, not
  commerce — a stall sells sugar, never engines.
- **R-004 (2026-07-18).** The Founder is dead and stays dead. Statues,
  fountains, and stories may invoke her; no beat casts her.
